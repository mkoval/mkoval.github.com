Who am I?

I am Michael Koval, a first year Ph.D. student in the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. My research interests broadly relate to robotic manipulation.artificial intelligence and computer vision to machine learning. In particular, I am interested in the manipulation problem. Over the summer, I am working with Mehmet Dogar and Sidd Srinivasa in the Personal Robotics laboratory in the Robotics Institute.

Previously, I was an undergraduate who double majored in Electrical and Computer Engineering and Computer Science at Rutgers University. During my time as an undergraduate, I worked with Michael Littman in RL3 on the topic of reinforcement learning. Additionally, I was heavily involved in the Vex and IGVC robotics competitions through my leadership of the IEEE Student Branch. This culminated in me becoming the project leader of Navi, Rutgers University's entry in the 2012 Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition.
