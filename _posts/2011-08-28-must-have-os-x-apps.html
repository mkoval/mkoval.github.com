Must Have OS X Applications

Foo

  • Adium: FOt's O
  • Alfred: More than all else, Alfred's polish and unbeatable team of developers makes it stand out amongst the crowd of other application launchers. Incidentally, Alfred's Powerpack is the best clipboard management utility that I've had the pleasure to use.
  • BetterTouchTool: One of the most attractive features of the unibody MacBook Pro is its massive glass multitouch trackpad. BetterTouchTool is a small utility that fills a huge gap in OS X: the ability to map commands to custom multitouch gestures.
  • Caffeine: This is by far the simplest utility on the list, but perhaps the one that I use most often. useful. Click Caffeine's status bar icon once to stop your computer's screen from dimming and from going to sleep. Click it again to go back to normal.
  • Dropbox: FOO
  • Fantastical: Google Calendar and iCal iCal in Snow Leopard are great for viewing calendars, but creating events is always cubersome. Using a minimalistic interface and natural language processing, Fantastical makes scheduling events effortless.
  • GitX: FOO
  • MacVim: FOO
  • Reeder: The best RSS aggregator for iOS is now one of the best-designed applications on OS X. Reeder syncs with Google Reader, so switching between devices is seamless.
